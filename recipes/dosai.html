Dosa

Description

A dosa is a thin, savoury crepe in South Indian cuisine made from a fermented batter of ground black gram and rice. Dosas are served hot, often with chutney and sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew). Dosas are popular in South Asia as well as around the world.

A mixture of rice and white gram that has been soaked in water for at least 4–5 hours is ground finely to form a batter. Some add a bit of soaked fenugreek seeds while grinding the batter. The proportion of rice to lentils is generally 3:1 or 4:1. After adding salt, the batter is allowed to ferment overnight, before being mixed with water to get the desired consistency. The batter is then ladled onto a hot tava or griddle greased with oil or ghee. It is spread out with the base of a ladle or a bowl to form a pancake. It can be made either thick like a pancake, or thin and crispy. A dosa is served hot, either folded in half or rolled like a wrap. It is usually served with chutney and sambar. The mixture of white grams and rice can be replaced with highly refined wheat flour or semolina.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal
  • Methi seeds
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Soak Rice and Dal: In separate bowls, soak 2 cups of rice and ½ cup of urad dal with ½ teaspoon of fenugreek seeds in enough water for about 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind: Drain the soaked rice and dal. First, grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth paste, adding water as needed. Then, grind the rice to a smooth consistency.
  3. Mix and Ferment: Combine the ground rice and dal batter in a large bowl. Add salt to taste and mix well. Cover the bowl and let it ferment overnight or for about 8-12 hours in a warm place until it rises and becomes bubbly.
  4. Heat the Griddle: Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or ghee.
  5. Spread the Batter: Pour a ladleful of the fermented dosa batter onto the center of the tawa. Using the back of the ladle, spread the batter in a circular motion to form a thin, even dosa.
  6. Cook: Drizzle a few drops of oil or ghee around the edges of the dosa. Cook until the edges start to lift, and the bottom becomes golden brown and crispy.
  7. Flip or Fold: If you prefer a crisp dosa, you can cook just on one side. For a softer dosa, flip it and cook the other side for a few seconds.
