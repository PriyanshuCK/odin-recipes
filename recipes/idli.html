Idli

Description

Idli is a type of savoury rice cake, originating from the Indian subcontinent, popular as breakfast foods in Southern India and in Sri Lanka. The cakes are made by steaming a batter consisting of fermented black lentils (de-husked) and rice.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Urad dal
  • Methi seeds
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Soak Rice and Dal: Soak 2 cups of rice and ½ cup of urad dal with ½ teaspoon of fenugreek seeds in separate bowls for about 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind: Drain the soaked rice and dal. Grind the urad dal and fenugreek seeds to a smooth paste, adding water as needed. Then, grind the rice until smooth.
  3. Mix and Ferment: Combine the rice and dal batter in a bowl. Add salt, mix well, and let it ferment overnight.
  4. Prepare the Steamer: Grease the idli molds with oil. Pour the fermented batter into the molds.
  5. Steam: Place the molds in a steamer and steam the idlis for 10-15 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the idli comes out clean.
  6. Serve: Allow the idlis to cool slightly before removing them from the molds. Serve hot with chutney and sambar.
