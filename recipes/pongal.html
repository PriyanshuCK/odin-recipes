Pongal

Description

Pongal is a popular South Indian dish made with rice and yellow moong lentils. It is a comfort food, traditionally served as breakfast or lunch. It is flavored with black pepper, cumin, ginger, and ghee, and is often served with coconut chutney and sambar.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Yellow moong dal
  • Black pepper
  • Cumin seeds
  • Ginger
  • Curry leaves
  • Ghee
  • Water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Roast Dal: Dry roast Â½ cup of yellow moong dal in a pan until it turns aromatic.
  2. Cook Rice and Dal: Rinse and combine 1 cup of rice with the roasted dal. Cook in a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water and salt until soft.
  3. Temper Spices: In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee. Add 1 teaspoon of black pepper, 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, grated ginger, and a few curry leaves. Fry until aromatic.
  4. Mix: Pour the tempered spices into the cooked rice and dal mixture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Serve: Serve the pongal hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
